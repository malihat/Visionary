Money Pit or Money Maker

June 25, 2019 by Lorem Ipsm

When shopping around for a home, most prospective home buyers prefer to purchase a house with the latest renovations. By doing so thousands of dollars can be saved in repairs and maintenance-related expenses. While purchasing a home with modern renovations sounds like a no brainer there are some critical objectives that must be met if one is to avoid the financial pitfalls.

Before buying a home prospective homebuyers need to make sure that the seller provides the buyer with all of the proper permits for those renovations and that they were reviewed by a licensed inspector.

Most sellers avoid these permits due to the costs associated with them. These additions be it electrical, plumbing, or additions require these permits and inspections. Without these proper permits, it can become a problem if the buyer decides to sell the home in the future.

It is important to remember that a home is a long term investment and while purchasing a home with the latest renovations will save money in the short term you would not want to sacrifice the return on your long term investment for short term savings.
