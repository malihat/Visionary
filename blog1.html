Foreclosure Failures

June 25, 2019 by Lance Smith

How foreclosure affects entire communities. When a family is foreclosed upon the damage is not only felt by those affected individuals. It affects the entire surrounding community. Foreclosure means that a neighborhood just lost a homeowner, and in many communities, with higher foreclosure rates, it is not uncommon to see 3-5 foreclosed homes on one street. At that point areas that were once thriving communities become unrecognizable and areas that are vulnerable often decline.

Without homeownership, a community loses its identity, and in lower-income areas, these foreclosed homes stay on the market even longer leaving vacant houses open to squatters and other illicit activities further distressing those communities.

Instead of only getting homeowners out of foreclosure what is needed is investment in these lower-income areas with high rates of foreclosure. Investors who are serious about buying those homes and putting families into those foreclosed homes to restore these communities. When families are living in homes that they own they put down roots and raise their families which provides the needed economic boost to local businesses and tax revenue needed to help the schools in those communities stay ahead in an increasingly competitive society and workforce.
